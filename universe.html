Knowing the Universe


Astronomy, the oldest science, is vital to humanity. Observing the stars allowed for the creation of calendars, essential for agriculture and social organization. It guided navigators and propelled the development of technologies such as those used in cameras, satellites, and medicine. Furthermore, it offers us a profound perspective on our place in the vast universe, stimulating science and philosophy.

Facts about Black Holes:

  • It is a region of spacetime with gravity so intense that nothing (not even light) can escape.
  • The Event Horizon is the point of no return.
  • They form from the gravitational collapse of massive stars.
Characteristic Description
Singularity The center, where all the mass is concentrated in a zero volume.
Common Types Stellar (mass of 3 to 100 Suns) and Supermassive (millions to billions of Suns).
"Hair" Characteristics that define a black hole (mass, angular momentum, and electric charge).

The Star's "Adulthood":

  • The star fuses Hydrogen into Helium in its core, generating energy.
  • This phase constitutes about 90% of the star's total lifespan.
  • Our Sun is a Main Sequence star, classifying as a G-type.
Feature Description
Energy Source Nuclear Fusion (H to He)
Luminosity Wide range, depending on mass (from very dim to very bright)
Temperature From 2,000 K (Red) to 50,000 K (Blue)

Swollen and Cool:

  • The star's outer layers expand massively, making it hundreds of times larger than its Main Sequence size.
  • Its surface temperature is lower, giving it a characteristic red or orange color.
  • It will eventually shed its outer layers to form a Planetary Nebula.
Feature Description
Core State Inert Helium core
Size Can engulf inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth)
Luminosity High, due to large surface area

The Stellar Remnant:

  • Extremely dense remnant; about the size of Earth but with the mass of the Sun.
  • No longer generates heat via fusion, but slowly cools down over billions of years.
  • It's supported against collapse by Electron Degeneracy Pressure.
Feature Description
Composition Mostly Carbon and Oxygen
Support Electron Degeneracy Pressure
Max Mass 1.4 Solar Masses (Chandrasekhar Limit)

The Common Star:

  • The smallest and coolest type of Main Sequence star.
  • Burns its Hydrogen fuel extremely slowly, giving it an estimated **lifespan of trillions of years**.
  • They are the most numerous stars in the Milky Way galaxy.
Feature Description
Mass Range 0.075 to 0.5 Solar Masses
Luminosity Very low (dim)
Longevity Trillions of years (virtually immortal)

The Three Kepler's Laws:

  • First Law (Orbits): Planets move in elliptical orbits, with the Sun at one of the foci.
  • Second Law (Areas): The radius vector from the Sun to a planet sweeps out equal areas in equal times.
  • Third Law (Periods): The square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis.
Law Name What it describes
1st Law of Orbits Orbit shape (elliptical)
2nd Law of Areas Variation of orbital velocity
3rd Law of Periods Relationship between period and average radius
